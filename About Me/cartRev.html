Review

Starting Point

At the start of the CART year, my knowledge of web development was very limited; I had previously taken a computer science course in 7th grade and learned basic HTML and CSS but I had forgotten it all. The first few HTML and CSS exercises refreshed my memory and I got a grip back on what I’d forgotten. The first big project (CSS Zen Garden) however really left me stuck. I felt so lost at all the selectors that I had to use to meet project requirements and felt like I couldn’t make any progress. With the power of persistence and w3schools I managed to push through this and make something I am really proud of.

The "Aha!" Moments

My HTML and CSS skills really started to click together and flourish with the PawPal project, I was able to easily make layouts and style them. Even with my newly flourished skills, I still had another threat: JavaScript. When learning JavaScript, we were tasked with making a to do list tracker. The main issue I had was my drag and drop function. This was the bane of my existence and I wanted to give up but I persevered and I collaborated with peers and asked them how they ended up doing theirs and finally I got mine to work./p>

The Professional Turn

As the year came to a close, we learned advanced tools like CSS Clamp(), scroll linked animations and JS Class manipulation. These were specifically used for our portfolio websites and our Hero’s journey project.
More on the portfolio website, this project is the culmination of everything I have learned over the course of this year. Both the technical skills like JavaScript and ‘soft’ skills like communication and persistence have improved who I am as a dev and shows that I am able to compete with others in this field.
